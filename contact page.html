Quick Essentials:

1. Emergency Action Guide:

i. Emergency Contact Buttons:

  • Women’s Helpline (National Commission for Women): 1091
  • All-India Women’s Helpline: 181
  • Police: 100
  • National Emergency Number: 112
  • Domestic Abuse Helpline: 181
  • Child Abuse Helpline: 1098

ii. Self defence tools to be carried:

  • Pepper Spray: It can cause temporary blindness, breathing difficulties, and severe discomfort giving time to escape.
  • Personal Alarm: A small, portable device that emits a loud siren (usually over 120 decibels) when activated, attracting attention and potentially scaring off attackers.
  • Tactical Pen: A pen that doubles as a self-defense weapon. It's made of tough metal and can be used for striking or writing in emergencies.
  • Taser: The device can deliver an electric shock to incapacitate an attacker temporarily.

These are easily available on Amazon

2. Safety Maps:

i. Identify safe zones:

  • Police stations
  • Hospitals or clinics
  • Well-lit, busy public areas like malls, cafes, or public tranportation hubs.

ii. Public Transport and Ride-Sharing Stops:

  • Bus stops
  • Metro or train stations
  • Taxi stands

iii. SOS and GPS Tracking:

  • Some areas have physical SOS kiosks or panic buttons in high-risk zones: Map their exact locations.
  • Share your real-time location with trusted contacts.
  • Mark safe or unsafe areas based on user reports or personal experience.
